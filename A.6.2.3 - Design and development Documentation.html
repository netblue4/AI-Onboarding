2. Responsible AI Objectives (A.9.3)
8
PrincipleObjectiveHow does design choices support responsible AI objectives.
9
Job PreservationAssess and mitigate potential negative impacts of the AI system on job roles and support workforce adaptation.
10
FairnessEnsure model performance is consistent and equitable across relevant demographic groups (e.g., age, gender, ethnicity).
11
TransparencyProvide clear, understandable, and timely explanations to users and stakeholders for AI system decisions and operations.
12
AccountabilityClearly assign ownership and responsibility for AI system outputs, decisions, and errors.
13
SafetyPrevent harmful outcomes to individuals or property resulting from the AI system's operation, particularly in safety-critical contexts.
14
PrivacyProtect personal data processed by the AI system, ensure confidentiality, and avoid unauthorized re-identification.
15
Human OversightEnsure appropriate human involvement and intervention, especially for high-risk decisions or unexpected AI system behavior.
16
EU AI Act Art 15 - Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity (New)Ensure the AI system meets documented accuracy levels, is resilient to attacks and faults, and includes adequate cybersecurity protections, as required by EU AI Act Article 15.
17
Customer Needs & ExpectationsEnsure the AI system meets the stated needs of the customer and manage expectations through clear communication of its capabilities and limitations.
18
Supplier Alignment & RequirementsEnsure that third-party components, data, or services used in the AI system meet the organization's contractual and responsible AI obligations.
