A.6.2.3 Design and Development Documentation

This document outlines the system and data design, and how design choices support responsible AI objectives.

1. System & Data Design Documents

Field Response / Details
System Architecture Diagram: A visual chart showing all the major components (e.g., data pipelines, APIs, model serving engine, user interface) and how they interact.
Data Preprocessing and Transformation Logic: A detailed description of all steps taken to clean, filter, augment, and transform the data before it's used for training. This is critical for reproducibility.
Model Specification: Details the type of model chosen (e.g., logistic regression, convolutional neural network), the rationale for choosing it over others, and its key parameters.

2. Responsible AI Objectives (A.9.3)

Principle Objective
How does design choices support responsible AI objectives.
Job Preservation Assess and mitigate potential negative impacts of the AI system on job roles and support workforce adaptation.
Fairness Ensure model performance is consistent and equitable across relevant demographic groups (e.g., age, gender, ethnicity).
Transparency Provide clear, understandable, and timely explanations to users and stakeholders for AI system decisions and operations.
Accountability Clearly assign ownership and responsibility for AI system outputs, decisions, and errors.
Safety Prevent harmful outcomes to individuals or property resulting from the AI system's operation, particularly in safety-critical contexts.
Privacy Protect personal data processed by the AI system, ensure confidentiality, and avoid unauthorized re-identification.
Human Oversight Ensure appropriate human involvement and intervention, especially for high-risk decisions or unexpected AI system behavior.
EU AI Act Art 15 - Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity (New) Ensure the AI system meets documented accuracy levels, is resilient to attacks and faults, and includes adequate cybersecurity protections, as required by EU AI Act Article 15.
Customer Needs & Expectations Ensure the AI system meets the stated needs of the customer and manage expectations through clear communication of its capabilities and limitations.
Supplier Alignment & Requirements Ensure that third-party components, data, or services used in the AI system meet the organization's contractual and responsible AI obligations.
