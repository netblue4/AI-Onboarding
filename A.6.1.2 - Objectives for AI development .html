Job PreservationDesign the AI system to augment human roles where feasible and identify functions requiring workforce adaptation.• AI Impact Assessment identifying potentially affected roles
• Design documents showing human-in-the-loop integration points

• Bias mitigation strategy documents and validation outcomes
• Data sourcing and representativeness analysis		Build for Fairness: Incorporate fairness metrics into the model development and validation pipeline. Select and apply appropriate bias mitigation techniques before finalizing the model.

TransparencyDevelop a system whose decision-making processes are understandable to developers, users, and stakeholders.• Documentation of selected explainability techniques (e.g., SHAP, LIME)
• Initial drafts of user guidance materials and FAQs
• Architecture diagrams and model cards		Design for Clarity: Build and document explainability features as part of the core system architecture. Prepare initial documentation to explain system logic.

AccountabilityEstablish clear lines of ownership for the design, development, and approval of the AI system.• Approved role/responsibility matrix (e.g., RACI) for the project team
• Documented sign-offs at key development milestones
• Traceability logs from requirements to code		Assign Ownership: Finalize and get approval for the RACI chart before the main development phase begins. Ensure all key design and testing decisions are logged and attributed.
7
SafetyDesign and build the AI system with integrated safety controls and fail-safe mechanisms.• Documented safety-specific risk assessments for the design
• Records of fail-safe mechanism testing in a development environment
• Architectural documents detailing safety features		Engineer for Safety: Conduct a safety-focused risk assessment during the design phase. Code and unit-test all safety-critical features and fail-safe mechanisms.
8
PrivacyEngineer the system to protect personal data by design and by default.• Completed Data Privacy Impact Assessment (DPIA) before deployment
• Documentation of data minimization and de-identification techniques used
• System architecture showing privacy-preserving data flows		Implement Privacy by Design: Finalize the DPIA based on the system's architecture. Implement and test data minimization and de-identification scripts and processes.
9
Human OversightDesign workflows that require human intervention for high-risk decisions and provide clear interfaces for interaction.• System design documents detailing human review/override workflows
• Defined criteria for when human intervention is required
• Usability testing reports on human oversight interfaces		Design Intervention Points: Define and document the specific triggers and criteria for mandatory human review. Build and test the user interfaces for oversight.

• Cybersecurity penetration test reports from the development environment		Validate Technical Performance: Establish and document accuracy baselines. Perform adversarial and fault tolerance testing to ensure resilience. Conduct pre-deployment security testing.

• User Acceptance Testing (UAT) plans and scripts
• Traceability matrix linking requirements to features		Build to Specification: Ensure every feature developed can be traced back to a specific user requirement. Create comprehensive UAT plans for sign-off.

Job PreservationMonitor the AI system's real-world impact on job roles and ensure support resources are effective.• Post-deployment role impact analysis reports
• Retraining program participation and completion rates
• Employee feedback surveys on transition support		Track Workforce Impact: Periodically reassess how automation affects roles post-deployment. Ensure retraining resources are delivered and adjust based on feedback.
17
FairnessEnsure the model's performance remains fair and equitable across demographic groups in a live environment.• Ongoing fairness monitoring reports from production data
• Logs of drift detection and remediation actions
• Internal audit records of live system fairness reviews		Monitor for Bias: Schedule and run regular fairness audits on the live system. Trigger and document remediation when performance drift is detected.
18
TransparencyProvide clear and timely explanations to users and stakeholders for live AI system decisions.• Logs of stakeholder queries and resolution responses
• Live user guidance materials and updated FAQs
• Training records for end-users on system interpretability		Handle Queries: Respond to user and stakeholder inquiries with clear, non-technical explanations. Maintain updated and accessible documentation for users.
19
AccountabilityEnsure clear responsibility for system outputs, incident management, and remediation in the operational environment.• AI incident register and root cause analysis reports
• Log of all human-in-the-loop interventions
• Documented remediation actions and their status		Run Incident Reviews: Use a structured playbook for post-incident debriefs. Keep ownership documentation current as operational teams evolve.
20
SafetyPrevent harmful outcomes by continuously monitoring the system's operational safety and human-system interactions.• Logs of safety-related incidents and near-misses
• Analysis of system-human interaction safety in production
• Periodic reports on the health of fail-safe mechanisms		Monitor Safety Incidents: Continuously monitor and log safety-related events and trigger an immediate review if a critical threshold is met.
21
PrivacyProtect personal data and ensure confidentiality throughout the system's operational lifecycle.• Regular audit logs of data access and processing activities
• Records of data subject requests (e.g., access, deletion)
• Up-to-date Data Privacy Impact Assessment (DPIA)		Audit Data Handling: Perform regular audits of data access logs to ensure compliance. Maintain the DPIA, reviewing it with every significant system change.
22
Human OversightEnsure that human oversight mechanisms are used effectively and as intended for live operations.• Logs of all human intervention and override activities
• Analysis reports on the frequency and outcomes of overrides
• Records of periodic testing of oversight workflows		Review Interventions: Regularly log and analyze all instances of human intervention to identify trends and assess the effectiveness of the oversight process.
23
EU AI Act Art 15Ensure the live AI system maintains its documented levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity.• Live accuracy monitoring reports and deviation logs
• Cybersecurity incident response plan and test records
• Production monitoring logs for faults and anomalies		Track Live Metrics: Monitor accuracy against established thresholds and log deviations. Perform periodic cybersecurity audits and maintain a tested incident response plan.
24
Customer NeedsEnsure the live AI system continues to meet customer needs and manage expectations effectively.• User feedback analysis reports (e.g., tickets, surveys)
• Customer satisfaction scores (e.g., NPS, CSAT)
• User Acceptance Testing (UAT) results for new releases		Manage Feedback Loop: Operate and monitor user feedback channels to gather insights on satisfaction. Validate that system performance remains aligned with user needs.
25
Supplier AlignmentEnsure third-party providers consistently meet their contractual and responsible AI obligations.• Service Level Agreement (SLA) monitoring reports
• Records of periodic supplier audits and performance reviews
• Updated supplier agreements and policy documentation		Conduct Supplier Reviews: Perform regular reviews of supplier performance against SLAs and responsible AI obligations. Ensure all agreements are kept current.
