Dataset Name / IDCategoryPreparation StepDescription & ExamplesRationale for ISO 42001
29
1. De-identification & AnonymizationMaskingReplacing parts of sensitive data with a generic character (e.g., 'XXX-XX-1234' for a Social Security Number).Demonstrates controls to protect personal data and reduce privacy risks.
30
1. De-identification & AnonymizationTokenizationReplacing a sensitive data element with a non-sensitive equivalent, referred to as a "token." The original data is stored in a secure vault.A strong security measure to prevent data breaches from exposing sensitive information.
31
1. De-identification & AnonymizationHashingUsing a cryptographic function to convert sensitive data into a fixed-size string of characters. It's a one-way process.Ensures data integrity and provides a secure way to verify data without exposing it.
32
1. De-identification & AnonymizationGeneralizationReducing the precision of data. For example, changing a specific age ('34') to an age range ('30-40') or a full date of birth to just the year.Reduces the risk of re-identification while retaining some analytical value.
33
2. Access Control & GovernanceData SegregationCreating physically or logically separate data stores for sensitive information, with strict access controls.Evidence of implementing access control policies, a key security principle.
34
2. Access Control & GovernanceAttribute-Based Access ControlApplying rules so that only users with specific attributes (e.g., role, department) can access certain data fields.Documents a granular approach to data security and the principle of least privilege.
35
3. Data MinimizationFeature PruningActively removing any data fields or columns that are not strictly necessary for the AI model's purpose.Shows adherence to data minimization principles, reducing the "attack surface" of the data.
36
3. Data MinimizationRow (Sample) ReductionRemoving records of individuals who have not given consent for their data to be used for AI training.Documents the process for respecting user consent and legal obligations (like GDPR).
